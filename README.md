AI Impact on Jobs – Power BI Dashboard
Overview
This project explores the impact of Artificial Intelligence on employment patterns, focusing on how AI influences job modification, displacement, salary changes, and industry-level automation risk. The dashboard transforms workforce data into clear, actionable insights for understanding future job market dynamics.

Objectives
Analyze the effect of AI on job roles (unchanged, modified, replaced)
Evaluate salary changes before and after AI adoption
Identify industries most exposed to automation
Measure workforce stability and displacement trends
Provide insights for workforce planning and strategic decision-making

Dataset
Total Records: 2,000 employees
Covers multiple industries including Finance, IT, Manufacturing, Education, Marketing, and Retail
Key attributes include job roles, salary (before & after AI), industry type, and job status post-AI adoption

Key Metrics
Total Employees: 2000
Job Modification Rate: 40.05%
Job Displacement Rate: 5%
Stable Workforce Rate: 54.7%
Average Salary Impact: +$4.49K

Key Insights
More than half of jobs remain unchanged (54.65%), indicating AI is currently more augmentative than disruptive.
A significant portion of roles (40%) are modified, highlighting evolving job responsibilities rather than full replacement.
Job displacement is relatively low (5%), suggesting gradual workforce transition instead of mass unemployment.
Industries such as IT and Finance show higher exposure to automation, while others remain moderately affected.
Salary analysis indicates a positive overall impact, especially for analytical and technical roles.

Dashboard Features
KPI cards for quick workforce overview
Job status distribution (unchanged, modified, replaced)
Industry-wise automation exposure analysis
Salary comparison (before vs after AI)
Interactive filters for deeper exploration

Tools & Technologies
Power BI (Data Visualization & Dashboarding)
Excel / CSV (Data Source)
DAX (Calculated measures & KPIs)

Business Value

This dashboard helps stakeholders:
Understand AI-driven workforce transformation
Identify roles and industries at risk of automation
Support strategic hiring and reskilling decisions
Evaluate salary impact trends in an AI-driven economy

Final Conclusion
The analysis indicates that Artificial Intelligence is transforming jobs rather than eliminating them outright. Most roles are either being modified or remain stable, while full displacement remains limited. However, the shift in job structure and skill requirements is significant, especially in technology-driven industries. Overall, AI is acting as a force of augmentation and evolution, reshaping workforce dynamics and increasing the value of analytical and technical skills.
